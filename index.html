  • 1

  • 3
    Use boiling water for the scald. Mix for at least 15–20 minutes.
  • 4
    Cool the scald — it must be fully and thoroughly cooled before continuing. Do not rush this step; incomplete cooling will ruin the batch.
    Tip: point a fan at the mixer bowl and periodically run the mixer to move the dough and ensure even cooling.

  • 1

Guidelines for baking, cleaning & packing

Floors

  • Sweep the entire Orban room floor thoroughly, including under tables and equipment.
  • Mop with a clean mop and food-safe cleaning solution.
  • Pay special attention to corners and edges where flour collects.
  • Allow floor to dry fully before leaving the room.

Table Surfaces

  • Discard any unused flour remaining on the table — do not return open flour back to the bag. Place in bin before wiping down surfaces.
  • Wipe down all table surfaces, counters, and work benches with a food-safe cleaner.
  • Remove any leftover flour, dough, or residue completely.
  • Wipe the stamp rack and rolling pin storage area.
  • Clean the inside and outside of all trays and cooling racks.

Machines & Equipment

  • Mixer: Remove bowl and dough hook, wash thoroughly. Wipe down exterior and base. Ensure no dough residue remains on the hook or bowl walls.
  • Proofer: Wipe interior walls and floor of the proofer with a damp cloth. Remove any moisture or residue.
  • Ovens: Once cooled, wipe interior surfaces. Check that no Orban pieces or crumbs remain inside.
  • Baking Trays: Wipe all baking trays thoroughly to remove any crumbs and residue — this is the most critical step. Pay close attention to tray edges and corners where crumbs collect. Store dry once cleared.
  • Return all stamps, rollers, and tools to their designated storage locations.

Table Setup — Layering Sequence

Layer the table in this order, bottom to top:

1 Plastic — base layer, protects the table
2 Blanket — cushioning layer
3 Sheet — clean linen layer
4 Towel — absorbs moisture
✝ Orban placed here — Hamal Orban in dedicated plastic bags
5 Towel — covers Orban
6 Sheet — clean cover layer
7 Plastic — top cover, seals from air and dust

Cooling First

  • Allow Orban to cool completely before packing — packing warm bread causes condensation and sogginess.
  • Cover loosely with a clean cloth while cooling to retain moisture without trapping steam.
  • Do not cool Orban outside the room or in open air — exposure to drafts or dry air will dry out the crust quickly and affect the texture.
  • Keep the Orban room door closed during cooling to maintain a stable, humid environment.

Packing

  • Handle each Orban gently — avoid pressing on the top stamp pattern.
  • Place Hamal Orban (the Communion loaf) separately at the front with extra care.
  • Pack each Orban individually in a clean bag or wrap to maintain freshness.
  • Label bags if multiple liturgies are being prepared (e.g., 1st vs 2nd Liturgy sizes).
  • Store packed Orban in a clean, dry location away from strong odors.

Storage Tips

  • Use within 24 hours for best quality.
  • If baking the night before, store at room temperature in sealed bags — do not refrigerate.

📦 Away Liturgy — Packing in Boxes

  • Use the designated transport boxes — these are the approved boxes used by the church for away liturgies.
  • Line the bottom of each box with a clean towel before placing any Orban inside.
  • Place Orban flat, stamp-side up. Orban may be stacked — place a clean towel between each layer to protect them.
  • Place Hamal Orban in its own dedicated box, clearly labelled, and keep it accessible at the top.
  • Transport boxes flat — never tilt or place heavy items on top. Assign someone to hold boxes steady in the vehicle.
  • Upon arrival, open boxes carefully and transfer Orban to the serving area using the same care as at church.

When to Make Extra

  • Friday night and Saturday night batches should account for extra Orban needed for Sunday church activities — Sunday School, youth meetings, and any special events.
  • Check with the Orban leaders group in advance if there are any extra requests for the weekend.

How Much Extra

  • For a standard weekend, add 10–20% more than the usual liturgy amount.
  • For special occasions (feast days, large gatherings), coordinate directly with the priest or deacon to determine the required count.
  • Use the Override Parameters on the Home screen to adjust the count and size before calculating your batch.

Timing

  • Start Friday night prep early enough so Orban are fully cooled and packed before midnight.
  • Saturday night batches should be baked and packed by 11 PM to ensure freshness for Sunday morning.
  • Always report flour and yeast inventory to the Orban leaders group after each bake.

Baking Time

  • Bake at 365°F for a total of 22 minutes.
  • After 5 minutes, remove and re-poke all holes, then return to finish.
  • Large Orban (>1000 g) may need 1–2 extra minutes — check color, not just time.

✅ Ideal: Lightly golden — smooth, even colour across the top. The Orban should be fully baked and soft, not crispy or hard on the bottom. The stamp pattern should remain visible and defined.

What to Watch For

  • Raw / Underdone: Pale cream colour, doughy texture, stamp pattern looks puffy and undefined. Return to oven and check every 2 minutes.
  • Ideal: Light even golden colour, firm but soft to touch, stamp pattern clear and set.
  • Overdone: Deeper brown, harder crust, slightly dry. Still usable but not preferred.
  • Burnt: Dark brown or black patches, hard crust, bitter smell. Not suitable for liturgy.
